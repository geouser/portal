To achieve its mission the Foundation established the Advisory board. The Advisory Board of the AA Foundation is the collegial body, the main function of which is to provide expertise in regard to developing and implementing the Foundation’s strategic plan for the benefit of artists and art community. The Advisory Board shares and supports the mission and goals of the Foundation, and contributes ideas, research and knowledge to strengthen the Foundation’s work.

Ruben Arevshatyan is an artist, art critic and independent curator. He is president of AICA-Armenia and teaches at the Contemporary Art Institute, Yerevan. In 1997–2004, he was artistic director of Hay-Art Cultural Center, Yerevan. In 2004–2013, he was lecturing in art history at the Arts Department of the Armenian Open University, Yerevan. He has published widely on contemporary art, architecture, and theory – mainly around topics of urban and cultural transformation focusing on Post-Socialist contexts. He is member of the editorial board of www.red-thread.org e-journal.

A Swiss citizen of Armenian origin, Mrs Cüberyan von Fürstenberg is a renowned international curator, winner of the Golden Lion for the best National Participation at the 56th Venice Biennale, for her curatorship of the Armenian Pavilion (2015), winner of the Swiss Grand Prix Meret Oppenheim, 2016.

A pioneer in the field, she is known for broadening the scope of contemporary art. She was one of the first curators to show active interest in non-European artists and established a signature multicultural approach to art.

Viktor Misiano was born in Moscow, Russia in 1957. From 1980 till 1990, he was curator of contemporary art at the Pushkin National Museum of Fine Arts in Moscow. From 1992 to 1997, he was director of the Center for Contemporary Art (CAC) in Moscow. He curated the Russian participation in the Istanbul Biennale (1992), the Venice Biennale (1995, 2003), the São Paulo Biennale (2002, 2004), and the Valencia Biennale (2001). He was on the curatorial team for Manifesta I in Rotterdam in 1996, and in 2010 – 2014, was chairman of Manifesta International Foundation.

Born and living in Jerusalem, Israel, curator and producer Jack Persekian is the founder and director of Anadiel Gallery and the Al-Ma’mal Foundation for Contemporary Art in Jerusalem. His previous positions include: director and head curator of the Palestinian Museum (2012–2015), director of the Sharjah Art Foundation (2009–2011), artistic director of the Sharjah Biennial (2007–2011), and head curator of the Sharjah Biennial (2004–2007).

Christina Steinbrecher-Pfandt was born in 1983 in Kazakhstan. She finished her studies at Maastricht University, the Netherlands, with a BA in International Business, and continued to study at Manchester University, UK where she graduated with a Master in Contemporary Art in 2007. In addition to her role as artistic director of Art Moscow fair (2009–2012) and Central House of Artists (Moscow, Russia, 2011–2012), she curated numerous exhibitions, including Unconditional Love at the 53rd Venice Biennale (2009), and projects at the 3rd and 5th Moscow Biennales of Contemporary Art (2009 and 2013).
